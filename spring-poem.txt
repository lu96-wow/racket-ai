Spring Morning

The buds awake from winter's sleep,
As gentle rains begin to weep.
The golden sun with warming grace,
Brings color to a sleepy face.

A robin sings upon the tree,
"Come, dance with me, O world, be free!"
The fragrance of the blooming rose,
Awakens all that winter froze.

The brook that once was cold and still,
Now dances down the rolling hill.
New leaves unfold, the earth is green,
The loveliest season yet is seen.

Oh, tender spring, with gentle hand,
You breathe new life across the land.
You paint the world in vibrant hue,
And make the weary heart feel new.
